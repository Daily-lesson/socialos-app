Privacy Policy

SocialOS — effective July 13, 2026

The short version: SocialOS is a local-first app. Your profile, content, tokens, and settings are stored in your own browser on your own device — there is no SocialOS account and no server-side database of your content. Data leaves your device only for the specific purposes listed below, and nothing is ever posted to any social platform without your explicit approval.

1. What SocialOS stores, and where

Everything you enter or import is stored locally in your browser (IndexedDB) on the device you use:

  • Profile details you type during setup (name, title, employer, goals, audience, topics, tone preferences, blackout dates, off-limits topics).
  • Social profile handles you link, and any publicly available profile information fetched for them.
  • Content you import — uploads from your device, files read from Google Drive, photos you pick from Google Photos, and text or links you paste.
  • Drafted posts, calendars, project notes, and engagement items.
  • OAuth access/refresh tokens for services you connect (Google and, if configured, LinkedIn, Reddit, or TikTok).

Clearing the app's site data in your browser, or using Settings → Reset All Data, permanently deletes all of it.

2. When data leaves your device

AI drafting and analysis (Anthropic Claude)

To draft posts, analyse content, and suggest replies, SocialOS sends the relevant text or image to Anthropic's Claude API through a small relay function we operate (hosted on Supabase). Before anything is sent, a local scrubbing step removes your employer name, client names, facility locations, proprietary terms, financial figures, and any custom blocked terms you configure. The relay holds the API key, forwards your request, and stores nothing. Anthropic processes the request under its own privacy policy.

Google Drive and Google Photos

Connecting Google is optional. If you tap "Sign in with Google," you authenticate on Google's own page — SocialOS never sees your Google password. The app requests two read-only scopes:

  • drive.readonly — lets SocialOS read your Drive files to suggest post content. It cannot create, modify, share, or delete anything in your Drive.
  • photospicker.mediaitems.readonly — lets SocialOS receive only the photos and videos you explicitly select in Google's picker. It can never browse or scan your photo library.

How this data is handled:

  • Your Google tokens are stored only on your device. The one-time sign-in handshake (exchanging Google's authorization code for tokens, refreshing them, and revoking them) passes through a stateless server function we operate, which keeps the app's OAuth client secret server-side and stores no tokens and no data.
  • File and photo content read from Google is stored locally on your device, and excerpts are sent (after the scrubbing step above) to the AI service for analysis as described in the previous section.
  • Google user data is never sold, never shared with any other third party, never used for advertising, and never used for any purpose other than the content-suggestion features you see in the app.
  • Disconnecting in Settings revokes SocialOS's access with Google and deletes the tokens from your device. You can also revoke access anytime at myaccount.google.com/permissions.

SocialOS's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Social platforms (LinkedIn, Reddit, TikTok, Facebook, Instagram)

  • Profile linking during setup reads publicly available profile data only (for example, public Reddit posts or a public TikTok display name).
  • If you connect a platform account for direct posting, you authenticate on that platform's own page and its tokens are stored only on your device. Because these platforms' APIs don't accept direct browser calls, requests pass through a stateless relay function that forwards them and stores nothing.
  • Nothing is ever posted, liked, or replied to on your behalf without your explicit approval of that specific item.

Automatic error reports and feedback

If the app hits a bug, a technical error report (error message, stack trace, page URL, browser user-agent string, and app version) is sent to our error-tracking service (Sentry) so we can fix it. These reports contain no content, tokens, profile data, or anything you've imported. Feedback you choose to submit through the in-app feedback button is stored so we can act on it.

Infrastructure

Like any website, the servers that deliver the app can see standard request metadata (such as your IP address) in their logs: GitHub Pages serves the app's files, Supabase runs the relay functions above, and Google Fonts serves the app's typefaces. None of these receive your app content.

3. What SocialOS does not do

  • No SocialOS account, sign-up, or server-side profile.
  • No advertising, no analytics trackers, no tracking cookies.
  • No selling or sharing of your data with third parties.
  • No posting without your explicit per-item approval.

4. Data retention and deletion

Because your data lives on your device, you control it directly: Settings → Reset All Data erases everything the app has stored; disconnecting a service revokes and deletes its tokens; clearing the site's data in your browser has the same effect as Reset. We retain nothing server-side to delete, other than transient function logs and the error reports described above.

5. Children

SocialOS is not directed at children under 13 and should not be used by them.
